High Molecular (HM) Plastic

What it is: HM (High Molecular Weight Polyethylene) is a tough, high-density form of plastic with superior tensile strength and resistance to stress cracking.

Applications: Used for creating sturdy and long-lasting products such as industrial containers, chairs, chemical storage cans, and heavy-duty packaging films.

Advantages: Excellent durability, superior chemical resistance, and longevity ‚Äî making it ideal for high-strength applications.

Low Density (LD) Plastic

What it is: LD (Low-Density Polyethylene) is a flexible and lightweight thermoplastic known for its clarity, softness, and resistance to moisture.

Applications: Commonly used to manufacture carry bags, shrink wraps, polythene sheets, and food-grade packaging films.

Advantages: Easy to mold, excellent flexibility, and ideal for light packaging and consumer-grade applications.

Colored Recycled Plastic

What it is: These colored granules are made from high-quality recycled polymers blended with pigments, offering both strength and visual appeal.

Applications: Used to create decorative, household, and commercial products such as chairs, containers, buckets, and toys that demand a specific color finish.

Advantages: Adds aesthetic versatility while retaining the strength and recyclability of the base material ‚Äî perfect for design-focused plastic manufacturing.
